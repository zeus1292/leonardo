# Passive Income Research

Research on building sustainable, mostly-passive income as a solo AI builder.

---

## Models That Work for Solo AI Builders

### 1. SaaS (Software as a Service)
- **Recurring revenue, scales well**
- Best for: tools with clear, repeated use cases
- Challenge: requires marketing + support investment
- Path to $1k MRR: ~20-50 customers at $20-50/mo
- Good fit if: you can get to product-market fit with <100 users

### 2. Usage-Based API / Tool
- **Pay as you go — low friction to start**
- Best for: AI capabilities that other devs want to embed
- Challenge: needs developer distribution, docs, reliability
- Examples: custom fine-tuned models, specialized AI endpoints

### 3. Template / Workflow Packs
- **One-time purchases, almost zero marginal cost**
- Best for: prompt packs, n8n flows, Claude projects, Notion templates
- Challenge: low price points, needs volume
- Path to $1k: 100 sales at $10 or 20 sales at $50

### 4. Information Product / Course
- **Leverage your AI PM expertise**
- Best for: teaching AI product skills, interview prep, career pivoting to AI PM
- Challenge: takes time to build, needs audience
- Akshay's angle: "How I went from PM to AI builder" or "AI PM interview bootcamp"

### 5. Productized Consulting (not fully passive but scales)
- **High value, predictable, can scale with AI tools**
- Best for: AI strategy audits, RAG implementation reviews
- Challenge: time-bound, not truly passive
- Can be a bridge while building SaaS

---

## Criteria for a Good Passive Income Idea (Akshay's filter)

1. **Willingness to pay is obvious** — people are already paying for something similar
2. **Solo-buildable MVP** — can ship v1 in 1-2 weeks with existing skills
3. **Distribution path exists** — Reddit, Twitter/X, ProductHunt, LinkedIn, niche communities
4. **AI is the wedge** — not just an AI wrapper, but genuinely better because of AI
5. **Recurring or high-ticket** — avoid one-time low-price without volume
